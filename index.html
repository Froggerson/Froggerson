Our World is dying...

Let's save it. Together

So what's up?

Let's get this out of the way: climate change is happening right now, and if we don't something, its effects will have a negative effect on the future. It can be scary to go about and change things by ourselves, but luckily for us UCSD offers a bunch of organizations that will help students "bend the curve", a phrase that refers to reducing the amount of CO2 emmissions we release on a year to year basis. Now is the perfect time to Let's find a community that will support you and our world!

Would simply joining a club actually help?

Of course it will! Even if you do not actively participate in fighting against climate change right now, finding an group that does will help you gain awareness about strategies that will make a difference. You'll make new friends and work with a group of people who know what they are doing. You don't need to participate in a rally or lead a protest to reverse the effects of climate change; you can teach others, make small changes to your lifestyle, get your friends involved, or even talk about it casually in conversation. College is a great time to do that, and nothing is better than meeting new people who believe in the same causes as you do! Let's take a look at what UCSD has to offer.

Join others at UCSD

Engineers for a Sustainable World

Interested in using your engineering skills for the greater good? With ESW, you and your team can research and build new sustainable technology, such as solar-powered batteries and human-powered generators! If building is not your thing, you can also focus on teaching elementary students about climate change through various mediums, such as video games or presentations.

Creating new technology can help us reach an "80% reduction in CO2 emmissions by 2050", while teaching others can spread awareness about the issue and get people to be more involved (Ramanathan, 4-29). In this way, ESW is really "bending the curve!"

UCSD Calpirg

If you thinking about getting more politically involved with climate change, this is the club for you. Calpirg runs grassroots campaigns, lobbies politicians, and more!

Political action is essential to fighting climate change. The government relies on the will of the people in order to operate. If we band together and let politicians know that climate is an urgent issue then the government can offer tax incentives for using sustainable techonologies, ban plastics, and regulate pollution which would lower CO2 emmisions on a much greater scale. Calpirg has already accomplished this in 2017, when they got the state of California to commit to 100% clean energy by 2045.

Learn More

Grow UCSD

Enjoy gardening? We do too! Cultivate UCSD's many community gardens with Grow! From planting trees to teaching people how to grow their own crops, Grow is committed to sustainability. Join a group of people who are committed to making the world greener, and make new friends on the way.

It takes a lot of fuel to transport food from place to place. By growing crops on UCSD's campus, we can reduce the amount of CO2 that would have otherwise come from trucks delivering fresh produce. Furthermore, the community gardens use compost from the dining halls, which reduce the amount of methane that would have otherwise been released if the food waste were to simply rot away.

Green Corps at UCSD

If you're concerned about both the environment and social justice, Green Corps is the place to be! They work to build technological innovations that are more energy efficient and improve the quality of life for everyone. Green Corps also has different tiers of membership, so you can choose how much time you want to dedicate to the club.

Climate change is not merely an environmental issue; it's a social one too. People who are most impacted by the effects of climate change come from low-income areas, even though they hardly contribute to CO2 emmissions. They do not have the means to migrate away from areas affected by heat waves and flooding. It is important to not forget about them as we fight against climate change.

Sustainability Resource Center

Get more involved on UCSD's campus with the Sustainability Recource Center! It is a great place for students to learn more about sustainability and participate in reducing UCSD's carbon emmissions.

Even if you can't come here often, the Sustainability Resource center offers seminars on the first Wednesday of the month if you ever want to drop by. They also recycle special items.

Food Recovery Network at UCSD

Want to help fight food waste in San Diego? Volunteer at the Food Recovery Network and deliver unwanted food to people who really need it!

Around 40% of food is wasted in the United States and that wasted food releases methane, a greenhouse gas, when it is left to rot in a landfill. By delivering food to hungry students, you'll be reducing the amount of methane released into the atmosphere and help people in need!

Inter-Sustainability Council UCSD

Be a leader on UCSD's campus! The Inter-Sustainability Council oversees all sustainability related organizations at UCSD. Work together with people from different organizations in order to create one collective movement and teach others about sustainability.

Organization is key to fighting climate change. Empowering people from different groups allows us to learn about how they fight againt climate change, no matter how small of action they may be, and adjust our strategies accordingly.
